About Me

In my second year of college, I was part of a team called WearTech, where we represented our school in a start-up app competition. I served as the Chief Technology Officer (CTO) and gained valuable experience in tech leadership and innovation.

I truly enjoyed this experience because it not only taught me valuable lessons but also expanded my network and connections.

In my third year of college , I was unexpectedly elected as the Vice President for SDG in our Supreme Student Council. It was my first time joining student leadership, and while the high position was initially overwhelming, I saw it as a great opportunity and embraced the challenge.

Although it was challenging, being a student leader turned out to be a truly rewarding experience. I discovered so much about myself and gained valuable insights through this journey. I also had the chance to meet diverse individuals, each with unique dreams and aspirations in life.

I also joined a Mobile Legends tournament at school, and my team was really strong—shoutout to my awesome classmates! We spent a week at the main campus in Alangilan for the tournament. Although we didn’t win, I had a lot of fun, laughed a lot, and grew as a team leader during that time.

In my fourth year, I focused more on my journey as a programmer since our capstone project was approaching. As the programmer in our group, I went through countless sleepless nights coding, but by God's grace, I was able to execute the codes and fix the errors successfully. There were even times when I forgot a semicolon and spent almost two hours searching for it—haha! It was an amazing journey.

By God's grace, we successfully defended our capstone project. It was such a fulfilling feeling to have developed an innovative solution through technology. It was incredibly stressful, though, and I ended up stress eating, which led to gaining some weight!
